Held every two years, alternating between venues in Europe and the United States, the tournament pits a team of twelve elite players from Europe against their American counterparts in a series of matches comprising foursomes, fourballs, and singles. The 2025 edition, hosted at Bethpage Black Golf Course in Farmingdale, New York, from 26 to 28 September, marked a return to American soil following Europe's triumphant victory in Rome in 2023 1. Background

The Ryder Cup's inception traces back to 1927, when it was established as a competition between teams representing Great Britain and Ireland and the United States. Conceived by American businessman Samuel Ryder, who was inspired by the growing popularity of match-play golf in the post-First World War era, the inaugural event was held at Gleneagles in Scotland 4. Samuel Ryder, a seedsman by trade with no prior golfing pedigree, funded a silver trophy that remains the prize today—a elegant chalice weighing approximately 3.5 kilograms and standing 45 centimetres tall 5. The early years were dominated by the United States, who won 12 of the first 15 contests, reflecting the disparity in professional golf's development across the Atlantic. Britain's team, often comprising amateurs alongside a handful of professionals, struggled against the more robust American lineup, which benefited from the sport's deeper integration into the cultural fabric of the United States.

A pivotal shift occurred in 1979 when the British team expanded to include continental European players, rebranding as Team Europe to foster greater competitiveness 6. This inclusion of talents from countries such as Spain, Sweden, and Germany injected fresh vitality, culminating in Europe's first victory in 1985 at The Brabazon Course in England, led by the legendary Tony Jacklin 7. Since then, the balance has tilted markedly; Europe has won 12 of the 22 Ryder Cups played from 1985 to 2023, including a record eight of the last 11 8. This resurgence can be attributed to several factors: enhanced scouting and development programmes across Europe, the unifying force of shared continental identity against the American powerhouse, and the tactical acumen of captains who emphasise team cohesion over individual stardom.

The tournament's format has evolved to heighten drama. Each Ryder Cup spans three days, with Day One featuring four foursomes and four fourball matches, Day Two mirroring this structure, and Day Three comprising 12 singles encounters 9. Foursomes involve pairs from each team alternating shots on a single ball, demanding synchronised strategy and trust, while fourballs allow each player to use their own ball, with the lower score per hole counting towards the team total 10. Points are awarded as 1 for a win and 0.5 for a halve, with 28 points available overall; the first team to 14.5 secures victory 11. This structure, refined over decades, ensures relentless pressure, as no single match can decisively sway the outcome, yet cumulative performances build inexorable momentum.

Historically, tensions have been a hallmark of the Ryder Cup, particularly on American soil. The 1991 event at Kiawah Island, dubbed the "War by the Shore," epitomised this intensity, with Bernhard Langer's missed putt on the final green sealing a narrow American win amid gale-force winds and psychological warfare 12. Subsequent editions, such as 1999 at Brookline and 2012 at Medinah, saw unsportsmanlike conduct from crowds, including chants and jeers that tested European resolve 13. Europe's 2018 victory at Le Golf National in France was marred by reports of unruly American fans, foreshadowing the charged environment anticipated for 2025 14. These incidents reflect broader cultural differences: American spectators often treat the event as a raucous party, contrasting with the more reserved European approach, leading to clashes that amplify the stakes 15.

The path to Bethpage Black in 2025 was paved by Europe's 2023 triumph in Rome, where they overcame a faltering start to win 16.5-11.5, thanks to rookie performances from Nicolai Højgaard and Bob MacIntyre 16. This success secured Luke Donald's captaincy for the defence, with Rory McIlroy serving as a vice-captain, his passion for the event well-documented 17. On the American side, captain Keegan Bradley, appointed after the 2023 debacle, sought redemption, selecting a roster blending veterans like Bryson DeChambeau with young stars such as Scottie Scheffler 18. Qualifying for the teams occurred through a points system based on performances in major championships and PGA Tour or DP World Tour events from the previous year, culminating in captain's picks announced in late August 2025 19. Europe's lineup included stalwarts like Jon Rahm, Tommy Fleetwood, and Viktor Hovland, alongside Justin Rose, whose selection at age 45 sparked debate given his semi-retirement status 20. The Americans countered with powerhouses including Xander Schauffele, Patrick Cantlay, and Collin Morikawa, aiming to leverage home support at a course known for its unforgiving layout.

Bethpage Black itself warrants contextualisation. Designed by A.W. Tillinghast and opened in 1936, this public course spans 7,468 yards with a par of 71, featuring notoriously long and narrow fairways, deep bunkers, and greens that demand pinpoint accuracy 21. It previously hosted the 2002 and 2009 US Opens, where Tiger Woods and Lucas Glover prevailed amid brutal conditions 22. For the Ryder Cup, the course's Black Course moniker—painted on a sign warning that it is "highly recommended only for highly skilled golfers"—sets a tone of intimidation 23. Weather forecasts for September 2025 predicted mild temperatures around 20 degrees Celsius with occasional showers, potentially softening the greens and favouring European players accustomed to variable conditions 24. This venue, located just 30 miles from Manhattan, drew an estimated 50,000 spectators daily, amplifying the pressure on both teams 25.

Subsequent holes, such as the 230-yard par-3 sixth over a vast chipping area, reward height control and spin, while the 600-yard par-5 12th demands precise navigation through tree-lined doglegs 28.

For team golf, Bethpage's design amplifies partnership dynamics. Fourball matches allow for aggressive play, yet the sloped greens, averaging 6,000 square feet with subtle breaks, require complementary short games from partners 30. The venue's public accessibility—admission via lottery for local residents—fosters an electric atmosphere, with grandstands erected along key holes like the 16th, a 408-yard par-4 finishing stretch 33.
