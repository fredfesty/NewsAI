Israel's Shifting Military Strategy in Gaza

A Comprehensive Analysis of Operations in Gaza City and Broader Gazan Campaign

Abstract

This article provides an in-depth examination of the recent directive ordering the Israeli military to halt its campaign in Gaza City, as reported by Israeli army radio on Saturday, October 4, 2025. It delves into the strategic implications of this order, its potential connection to the first phase of a plan attributed to Donald Trump aimed at ending the conflict and securing the release of hostages, and the ongoing Israeli military operations within the Gaza Strip. The analysis will explore the historical context of the conflict, the military objectives pursued in Gaza City, and the broader operational posture of the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) in the territory. Furthermore, it will investigate the stated rationale behind the directive to reduce operations in Gaza City to "the minimum," with troops purportedly focused on defensive strikes, and assess the potential impact of these changes on the humanitarian situation and the prospects for de-escalation. The article aims to provide a politically objective and detailed understanding of these developments, drawing upon extensive research from reputable sources.

Introduction

The landscape of the Israeli-Palestinian conflict is one of perpetual flux, marked by shifts in military strategy, political objectives, and international diplomatic efforts. A recent development, reported by Israeli army radio on Saturday, October 4, 2025, indicates a significant directive from Israel's political leadership to halt the military campaign within Gaza City. This order, while seemingly a de-escalation in one specific urban theatre, is reportedly intertwined with preparations for the initial phase of a broader plan, purportedly spearheaded by former US President Donald Trump, designed to bring an end to the ongoing hostilities and facilitate the release of hostages. The Israeli military has stated it is directed to "advance readiness" for this initial phase, suggesting a strategic recalibration. Concurrently, operations continue across the Gaza Strip, with the reported reduction in Gaza City's offensive operations to "the minimum," focusing troops on defensive strikes. This article seeks to dissect these layered developments, providing a comprehensive and objective analysis of the military directive concerning Gaza City, its potential linkages to proposed peace initiatives, and the overarching context of Israel's military engagement within the Gaza Strip. It will explore the background, strategic rationales, operational nuances, and potential implications of these evolving circumstances, aiming to offer a clear and detailed understanding to a readership with no prior assumed knowledge of the topic.

Background

To comprehend the significance of the reported halt in military operations in Gaza City, it is crucial to establish the historical and geopolitical context of the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, with a particular focus on Gaza. The Gaza Strip, a densely populated Palestinian territory on the eastern coast of the Mediterranean Sea, has been a focal point of this enduring conflict for decades. Its history is complex, marked by periods of Egyptian and Israeli control, blockades, and recurrent outbreaks of violence.

Following the 1967 Six-Day War, Israel occupied the Gaza Strip along with the West Bank and East Jerusalem. In the Oslo Accords of the 1990s, a degree of Palestinian self-governance was established, with the Palestinian Authority (PA) gaining administrative control over parts of Gaza. However, Israel maintained significant control over borders, airspace, and maritime access. The situation evolved dramatically in 2005 when Israel unilaterally disengaged from Gaza, withdrawing its troops and dismantling settlements. This disengagement was intended to improve security for Israel and transfer governance to the Palestinians 2.

The subsequent year, 2006, saw the election of Hamas, an Islamist militant organisation designated as a terrorist group by several countries including the United States and the European Union, to lead the PA. This electoral outcome was not recognised by Israel or much of the international community, leading to political division within the Palestinian territories, with Hamas eventually seizing full control of Gaza in 2007 4. This consolidation of power by Hamas led to a stringent blockade imposed on Gaza by Israel and Egypt, citing security concerns and the need to prevent Hamas from acquiring weapons. The blockade has had severe humanitarian and economic consequences for the population of Gaza 5.

Since 2007, the Gaza Strip has been the site of multiple large-scale military confrontations between Israel and Hamas, alongside other militant factions. These conflicts, often initiated by rocket attacks from Gaza into Israel and subsequent Israeli military responses, have resulted in significant casualties on both sides, extensive destruction of infrastructure in Gaza, and a deepening humanitarian crisis. Major escalations occurred in 2008-2009 (Operation Cast Lead), 2012 (Operation Pillar of Defence), 2014 (Operation Protective Edge), and again in subsequent years, including the extended conflict of late 2023 and early 2024 7.

The specific focus on Gaza City within this context is noteworthy. As the largest urban centre in the Gaza Strip, Gaza City has historically been a hub of both civilian life and, according to Israeli military assessments, a significant operational base for Hamas and other militant groups. Military campaigns in urban environments are inherently complex, involving a high density of civilian population, intricate underground infrastructure (including tunnels) 8, and dispersed militant strongholds. The challenge for any military operating in such a setting is to degrade militant capabilities while minimising harm to non-combatants and civilian infrastructure, a challenge that has proven particularly acute in Gaza 10.

The reference to a plan by Donald Trump, aimed at ending the war and releasing hostages, requires contextualisation within the broader diplomatic landscape. Throughout various administrations, efforts have been made to broker peace agreements or de-escalate conflicts between Israelis and Palestinians. The specific details of any plan attributed to Trump would need to be evaluated against the history of such initiatives and the current geopolitical climate. The assertion that Israel is preparing for the "first phase" suggests a structured approach to conflict resolution or de-escalation, implying a sequence of actions and potential agreements.

The directive to reduce operations in Gaza City to "the minimum" and focus on "defensive strikes" signals a potential shift in tactical approach. This could be motivated by various factors, including international pressure to reduce civilian casualties, a reassessment of military objectives, the achievement of certain strategic goals, or indeed, as suggested, alignment with a broader diplomatic framework. Understanding these motivations requires examining the stated objectives of the Israeli military and government, as well as the broader regional and international dynamics at play. The ongoing nature of strikes across the territory, however, indicates that the cessation of hostilities is not universal, suggesting a continued military engagement beyond the confines of Gaza City itself. This nuanced picture highlights the complexity of the current situation and the intricate interplay of military actions and diplomatic overtures.

Military Campaign in Gaza City: Objectives and Execution

The military campaign in Gaza City has, over successive conflicts, been characterised by a dual objective: to dismantle the military infrastructure of Hamas and other militant groups, and to degrade their operational capacity. Gaza City, being the administrative and population centre of the Strip, also hosts significant Hamas leadership, command centres, and extensive tunnel networks which the military asserts are used for logistical support, weapons storage, and movement of fighters 8. Israeli military operations in urban environments like Gaza City are inherently challenging due to the high population density, the presence of civilian infrastructure often intertwined with military targets, and the deliberate use of civilian areas by militant groups for operational purposes, as alleged by Israeli authorities 10.

Historically, operations in Gaza City have involved ground incursions, aerial bombardments, and targeted strikes. The objective has been to penetrate Hamas strongholds, neutralise combatants, destroy rocket launchers, and uncover and destroy tunnels. The execution of these operations requires sophisticated intelligence gathering and precise targeting to mitigate civilian harm. However, the effectiveness of such measures in densely populated urban settings has been a subject of intense debate and scrutiny, with international bodies and human rights organisations frequently raising concerns about the disproportionate impact on the civilian population 1011.

The reported order to halt the campaign in Gaza City suggests a recalibration of these objectives or methods. This could stem from several factors. Firstly, it might indicate that the military has achieved its primary objectives in the city, such as degrading Hamas's command and control capabilities or destroying a significant portion of its tunnel infrastructure. Secondly, it could be a response to international pressure or a desire to de-escalate tensions in a highly sensitive urban area to avoid further civilian casualties, which have been a major point of international criticism 10. Thirdly, and as suggested by the reporting 1, this directive might be a strategic move to facilitate or align with a diplomatic process aimed at hostage release and a broader cessation of hostilities. By reducing offensive operations in a core urban centre, Israel might be creating space for negotiations or demonstrating a willingness to de-escalate in anticipation of reciprocal actions.

The statement that operations are to be reduced to "the minimum" with troops focused on "defensive strikes" implies a shift from offensive manoeuvres to a more static, reactive posture within Gaza City. This could involve maintaining positions, responding to immediate threats, and preventing any resurgence of militant activity from within the city. Defensive strikes would typically be actions taken in direct response to an attack or imminent threat, rather than proactive offensive actions. This suggests a change in the tempo and nature of military engagement within the city, moving away from large-scale operations designed to clear and control areas towards a posture of containment and immediate threat response.

The continued strikes on the broader Gaza Strip, however, indicate that the halt is specific to Gaza City and not a general cessation of military activities. This distinction is crucial. It suggests that military objectives elsewhere in the Gaza Strip, potentially in less densely populated areas or in pursuit of different strategic aims, remain active. These ongoing operations could include targeting rocket launch sites, eliminating militant leaders in other areas, or disrupting supply lines. The precise nature and scope of these continued strikes are not detailed in the initial report but underscore that the conflict, in its entirety, is not concluded. This dual approach – de-escalation in one specific, high-profile urban area while maintaining operations elsewhere – highlights the intricate and often contradictory nature of modern warfare and conflict management.

The Proposed Trump Plan and Hostage Release Initiative

The reported directive to halt military operations in Gaza City is explicitly linked to preparations for the "first phase" of a plan attributed to former US President Donald Trump, designed to end the war and secure the release of all hostages 1. While the specific details of such a plan may not be publicly available in their entirety, or may be in the nascent stages of diplomatic discussion, the mention of Donald Trump in relation to conflict resolution in the Middle East carries significant weight, given his administration's involvement in the Abraham Accords and various diplomatic initiatives in the region.

The involvement of a former US president in mediating a ceasefire or proposing a peace plan suggests an attempt to leverage high-level diplomacy, potentially outside of current governmental structures, to break a stalemate. Such initiatives often emerge when conventional diplomatic channels appear to be faltering, or when a prominent figure with established relationships in the region can command attention and facilitate dialogue. The emphasis on the "first phase" implies a structured, phased approach to de-escalation and resolution. This could involve an initial agreement on a temporary ceasefire, followed by exchanges of prisoners or hostages, and potentially steps towards a more comprehensive political settlement.

The release of hostages is a paramount objective for Israel, particularly following incidents where individuals have been taken captive by militant groups. Any plan seeking to end the war would almost certainly need to address this issue as a primary component. Hamas and other militant factions have also, at various times, indicated a willingness to engage in hostage exchanges as part of broader agreements. The success of any such plan would hinge on the willingness of all parties involved – Israel, Hamas, and potentially other Palestinian factions – to commit to its terms.

The nature of a "plan to end the war" would typically encompass multiple elements beyond hostage release. These could include establishing a durable ceasefire, addressing humanitarian concerns, outlining a framework for post-conflict governance in Gaza, and potentially exploring pathways towards a longer-term political solution to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. The phased approach suggests a step-by-step process, where initial successes could build confidence and momentum for subsequent stages.

The reference to "advancing readiness" by the Israeli military for the first part of the Trump plan indicates that the military is aligning its operational posture with the potential diplomatic developments. This could involve preparing for troop movements, establishing buffer zones, or undertaking specific actions that are prerequisites for the initial phase of the plan, such as ceasing offensive operations in certain areas. The military's role in implementing any ceasefire or hostage release agreement is critical, requiring coordination and adherence to agreed-upon protocols.

The feasibility and potential success of such a plan would depend on numerous factors. These include the level of consensus among Israeli political factions, the willingness and ability of Hamas to adhere to an agreement, the nature of international guarantees and support, and the underlying political will to achieve a lasting resolution. The history of Israeli-Palestinian negotiations is replete with instances where well-intentioned plans have faltered due to a lack of trust, internal political divisions, or external spoilers. Therefore, while the announcement of such a plan and the military's preparatory steps are significant, their ultimate outcome remains subject to the complex dynamics of the conflict.

Ongoing Strikes and Military Posture in the Gaza Strip

While the directive to halt the campaign in Gaza City represents a specific tactical adjustment, the report explicitly states that Israel continues to conduct strikes on the Gaza Strip 1. This indicates that the military operations are not entirely ceasing but are being reconfigured. The rationale behind maintaining strikes elsewhere in the territory, while de-escalating in Gaza City, requires careful consideration.

Several possibilities exist for the continuation of strikes. Firstly, Israel may be pursuing strategic objectives in areas outside of Gaza City that it deems critical. This could include targeting military infrastructure, rocket launch sites, weapons caches, or militant leadership located in other parts of the Strip. Such operations might be considered less sensitive in terms of civilian impact or more crucial for Israel's immediate security concerns.

Secondly, the ongoing strikes could be part of a broader military strategy to maintain pressure on militant groups throughout Gaza, even while engaging in de-escalation in a specific urban centre. This pressure might be intended to weaken Hamas's overall capacity, disrupt its ability to regroup or retaliate, or create conditions conducive to the success of diplomatic efforts.

Thirdly, the strikes could be a direct response to continued rocket fire or other attacks originating from Gaza. If militant groups continue to launch projectiles into Israel, the Israeli military would likely maintain its right to respond in accordance with its operational doctrines. The focus on "defensive strikes" in Gaza City may not preclude offensive or retaliatory strikes in other areas if deemed necessary for security.

The term "strikes" itself can encompass a range of military actions, from aerial bombardments and missile strikes to artillery fire and special forces operations. Without further details, it is difficult to ascertain the precise nature and intensity of these ongoing operations. However, their continuation signifies that the conflict, in a broader sense, is still active, albeit with a potentially altered geographical focus or intensity.

This dual strategy—de-escalation in Gaza City coupled with continued strikes elsewhere—presents a complex operational picture. It suggests a calculated approach where specific theatres are being managed differently based on evolving strategic and diplomatic considerations. The international community will likely be observing these ongoing strikes closely, particularly concerning their impact on the civilian population and adherence to international humanitarian law 10. The effectiveness of this differentiated approach in achieving both military objectives and facilitating a peaceful resolution remains to be seen.

Implications for the Humanitarian Situation

The reported shift in Israeli military operations in Gaza, specifically the directive to halt the campaign in Gaza City while continuing strikes elsewhere, carries profound implications for the already dire humanitarian situation in the Gaza Strip 6. Gaza, a territory with one of the highest population densities in the world, has been under a severe blockade for years, exacerbating pre-existing challenges related to access to clean water, food, healthcare, and basic infrastructure.

The cessation of offensive operations in Gaza City, a major urban centre, could potentially lead to a reduction in civilian casualties and damage to infrastructure within that specific area. This would be a welcome development, given the widespread destruction and loss of life that have characterised previous military engagements in densely populated urban environments 10. A halt to ground incursions and heavy bombardment in Gaza City could alleviate immediate suffering for hundreds of thousands of residents, allowing for some degree of respite and potentially facilitating humanitarian aid delivery to this critical area.

However, the continuation of strikes across the rest of the Gaza Strip presents a significant caveat. If these strikes result in civilian casualties, damage to essential services, or further displacement of populations, the overall humanitarian benefit of de-escalation in Gaza City could be diminished. The fragmented nature of the conflict, with operations continuing in other areas, means that the risk to civilian life and well-being persists. The ability of humanitarian organisations to access affected populations and provide life-saving assistance will continue to be a critical challenge, potentially complicated by ongoing military activities.

The success of any humanitarian relief efforts will also be contingent on the broader context of the conflict and the progress of diplomatic initiatives. A sustained ceasefire, facilitated by the plan mentioned in the reports 1, would be essential for allowing for more comprehensive and effective humanitarian response. This would include the repair of damaged infrastructure, the restoration of essential services, and the provision of long-term support to a population that has endured immense hardship.

Furthermore, the role of external actors, including international humanitarian organisations and donor countries, will remain crucial. Ensuring unimpeded access for humanitarian aid, advocating for the protection of civilians and civilian infrastructure, and providing substantial financial and material support will be vital in mitigating the humanitarian consequences of the conflict 6. The potential for a phased de-escalation, as suggested by the "first phase" of the Trump plan, could offer opportunities for increased humanitarian access and the beginning of reconstruction efforts, but this is entirely dependent on the successful implementation and adherence to any agreements reached.

The psychological toll on the civilian population of Gaza, subjected to years of conflict, blockade, and displacement, is also a significant humanitarian concern. Any steps towards a lasting peace and a reduction in violence would offer much-needed relief and the possibility of rebuilding lives and communities. The current developments, while indicating a potential shift in military tactics, underscore the ongoing need for a comprehensive approach that prioritises the protection of civilians and addresses the fundamental humanitarian needs of the people of Gaza.

Analysis of Strategic Intent and Political Objectives

The directive to halt the campaign in Gaza City, while continuing strikes elsewhere, suggests a complex interplay of military objectives, political considerations, and potential diplomatic overtures. Understanding the strategic intent behind this recalibration requires examining the motivations of the Israeli political and military leadership.

One primary strategic objective for Israel has consistently been to degrade the military capabilities of Hamas and other militant groups, thereby enhancing its own security and preventing attacks. The campaign in Gaza City, being a densely populated urban area and a perceived stronghold of these groups, would have been undertaken with this objective in mind. The decision to halt or significantly reduce operations there could signal that certain military goals have been achieved, such as disrupting Hamas's command and control structures or destroying key infrastructure. Alternatively, it might reflect a strategic re-evaluation, perhaps acknowledging the diminishing returns of offensive operations in such a challenging environment or the increasing international scrutiny associated with high civilian casualties 10.

The explicit link to a plan attributed to Donald Trump suggests a significant political dimension to this military adjustment 1. If such a plan is indeed in play, and Israel is preparing for its initial phase, the halt in Gaza City could be a pre-emptive measure or a demonstration of good faith to facilitate negotiations. This implies that the political leadership views the potential diplomatic outcome as a more advantageous path forward than continued intensive military operations in that specific area. The release of hostages is a critical political imperative for any Israeli government, and any plan that offers a credible pathway to achieving this would carry substantial political weight.

The continuation of strikes in other parts of Gaza indicates that broader strategic objectives may still be in force. This could include maintaining pressure on militant groups, preventing the reorganisation of forces, or responding to ongoing threats. It suggests that Israel's security calculus involves maintaining a degree of military leverage across the entire Gaza Strip, even as it modifies its approach in specific locations. This nuanced approach might be designed to signal strength and resolve while simultaneously exploring diplomatic avenues.

Furthermore, the decision to reduce operations in Gaza City could be influenced by international pressure and the desire to mitigate negative global perceptions. The high number of civilian casualties in previous urban conflicts has drawn significant international condemnation, impacting Israel's diplomatic standing 10. A de-escalation in a highly visible urban centre like Gaza City might be intended to address these concerns and improve Israel's international image, particularly if it is perceived as a step towards peace.

The political leadership's communication regarding these decisions is also a factor. By framing the military directive within the context of a broader peace plan and hostage release initiative, the government aims to garner domestic support and project an image of proactive engagement in resolving the conflict. The emphasis on "defensive strikes" in Gaza City serves to frame the military's role as one of security and protection rather than unprovoked aggression.

In essence, the strategic intent appears to be a multifaceted approach that balances military objectives with political and diplomatic aspirations. It suggests a willingness to adapt operational strategies in response to evolving circumstances, including the potential for a negotiated settlement. However, the success of this strategy will depend on the adherence of all parties to any agreements reached, the effectiveness of the diplomatic framework, and the continued management of security concerns across the entire Gaza Strip.

Future Prospects and Potential Scenarios

The recent developments surrounding Israel's military operations in Gaza City and the broader Gaza Strip open up several potential scenarios for the future trajectory of the conflict. The directive to halt the campaign in Gaza City, coupled with preparations for the initial phase of a purported Trump plan for hostage release and war cessation, introduces an element of potential de-escalation and diplomatic engagement that has been elusive for extended periods 1.

One optimistic scenario involves the successful implementation of the "first phase" of the Trump plan. If this phase includes a durable ceasefire, a significant exchange of hostages for prisoners, and the unimpeded flow of humanitarian aid, it could create a foundation for further de-escalation. This could potentially lead to subsequent phases of the plan that address more complex issues, such as long-term security arrangements, reconstruction efforts, and pathways towards a political resolution. In this scenario, the reduction of military operations in Gaza City would be seen as a critical confidence-building measure that paved the way for a more comprehensive peace process.

A more cautious scenario anticipates a temporary lull in offensive operations in Gaza City, with continued, albeit perhaps reduced, military activity elsewhere in the Strip. The "defensive strikes" posture in Gaza City might be maintained, but the ongoing strikes in other areas could continue to fuel tensions and risk further escalation. This scenario would depend on the willingness of militant groups to cease attacks, including rocket fire into Israel, and on Israel's assessment of ongoing threats. The success of the Trump plan, in this context, might be limited to achieving a short-term reduction in violence rather than a lasting resolution.

A less optimistic scenario involves the breakdown of diplomatic efforts or the failure of the Trump plan to gain traction. If the initial phase falters, or if key parties do not adhere to its terms, Israel might revert to more aggressive military operations in Gaza City and across the Strip. This could be triggered by continued rocket attacks, perceived provocations by militant groups, or a strategic decision by the Israeli government to pursue a military solution more forcefully. Such a scenario would likely lead to a renewed cycle of intense conflict, further devastation in Gaza, and increased international condemnation 10.

The role of regional and international actors will be crucial in shaping these scenarios. The United States, Egypt, Qatar, and other nations have historically played significant roles in mediating ceasefires and facilitating negotiations. Their continued engagement, combined with pressure on all parties to de-escalate and engage constructively, will be vital. The United Nations and international humanitarian organisations will also play a critical role in monitoring the situation, providing aid, and advocating for the protection of civilians 6.

The internal political dynamics within both Israel and the Palestinian territories will also heavily influence future prospects. The stability of the Israeli government, the unity or division within Palestinian leadership, and the public sentiment on both sides will all play a part in determining the feasibility of any peace process.

Ultimately, the future prospects hinge on the complex interplay of military realities, political will, and diplomatic efficacy. While the reported shift in strategy offers a glimmer of hope for de-escalation and a potential path towards resolving the hostage crisis, the deep-seated issues underlying the conflict remain formidable challenges. A sustainable resolution will likely require sustained diplomatic engagement, a genuine commitment to de-escalation from all parties, and a comprehensive approach that addresses the humanitarian needs and political aspirations of both Israelis and Palestinians.
