The First Farewell

Thomas Skinner's Brief but Memorable Stint on Strictly Come Dancing 2025

Article created and last updated on: Monday 06 October 2025 08:30

Abstract

The 2025 series of the popular BBC dance competition, Strictly Come Dancing, has seen its first elimination. Businessman and television personality Thomas Skinner, partnered with the returning professional dancer Amy Dowden, became the first couple to depart the ballroom. Their journey was cut short following a dance-off against former England rugby player Chris Robshaw and his partner Nadiya Bychkova. Despite a spirited effort, Skinner's performances failed to garner sufficient scores from the judges or the public to keep him in the competition. The elimination was particularly poignant given Dowden's recent return to the show after a significant period of illness.

Key Historical Facts

  • Thomas Skinner was born in Romford, London, on 6 February 1991.
  • Skinner first gained public prominence on the fifteenth series of The Apprentice.
  • Skinner began his entrepreneurial career in market trading at the age of 16.
  • Professional dancer Amy Dowden joined the Strictly Come Dancing cast in 2017.
  • Dowden was diagnosed with grade three breast cancer in May 2023.

Key New Facts

  • Thomas Skinner and Amy Dowden were the first couple eliminated from the 2025 series.
  • They were eliminated after a dance-off against Chris Robshaw and Nadiya Bychkova.
  • Their Week One dance, a Paso Doble, received a total of 16 points.
  • Their Week Two Salsa to "Bonkers" scored only 13 points from the judges.
  • The judges' decision to save Chris Robshaw and Nadiya Bychkova was unanimous.

Introduction

The perennial appeal of Strictly Come Dancing lies in its blend of spectacle, celebrity endeavour, and the inherent drama of competition. Each series introduces a new cast of famous faces, each embarking on a transformative journey from novice to, in some cases, proficient dancer. The early weeks of the competition are often characterised by a steep learning curve, with contestants and their professional partners working intensively to master the intricate techniques of ballroom and Latin dance. The first elimination is always a significant moment, setting the tone for the remainder of the series and underscoring the high stakes involved. The 2025 season was no exception, with the departure of Thomas Skinner marking the first competitive milestone.

Thomas Skinner: The Entrepreneurial Spirit

Thomas Skinner, born on 6 February 1991, in Romford, London, is a figure who first came to public prominence as a candidate on the fifteenth series of the reality television show, The Apprentice 16. Known for his "cheeky chappy" persona and his "Delboy" style approach to business tasks, he quickly became a memorable contestant 21. His background is rooted in market trading, a career he embarked upon at the age of 16 16, 20. This early start in the world of commerce shaped his entrepreneurial drive, leading to the establishment of businesses such as The Fluffy Pillow Company and Bosh Beds 17, 21. Skinner's television appearances have not been limited to The Apprentice; he has also participated in programmes such as 8 Out of 10 Cats and Celebrity Masterchef 16. His public persona is further amplified by a significant social media presence, where he is known for his "BOSH!" catchphrase and for sharing videos of his daily life, including his much-talked-about breakfasts 16, 19.

Amy Dowden: A Resilient Return

Amy Dowden, a professional ballroom and Latin American dancer from Caerphilly, Wales, has been a popular member of the Strictly Come Dancing professional cast since she joined the show in 2017 30. Her career has been marked by both professional success and personal challenges. In May 2023, Dowden was diagnosed with grade three breast cancer, a development that necessitated a period of intensive treatment, including a mastectomy and chemotherapy 30, 38. This forced her to withdraw from the 2023 series of the competition 40. Her journey through treatment and recovery was documented in the BBC film, "Strictly Amy: Cancer and Me," which offered an intimate insight into her experiences 30, 40. In February 2024, she announced that tests had shown "no evidence of disease" 36, 40. Her return to Strictly Come Dancing for the 2025 series was therefore a highly anticipated and emotional moment, both for her and for the show's viewers 8, 13, 28.

The Early Rounds: A Tale of Two Dances

The first two weeks of Strictly Come Dancing are a crucial period for the contestants. With no public vote in the first week, the judges' scores are the sole determinant of the initial leaderboard. These scores are then carried over to the second week, where they are combined with the results of the first public vote to determine the two couples who will face the dance-off 27, 42.

Week One: The Paso Doble

For their first performance, Thomas Skinner and Amy Dowden took to the floor with a Paso Doble 27. The dance, known for its dramatic and powerful characterisation, proved to be a challenge for the novice dancer. The judges awarded the performance a total of 16 points, placing them towards the lower end of the leaderboard 3, 10, 12. This initial score immediately put them in a precarious position, highlighting the significant ground they would need to make up in the second week of the competition 27.

Week Two: The Salsa and the Slide

In the second week, the couple performed a Salsa to the track "Bonkers" by Dizzee Rascal and Armand van Helden 2, 22, 26. Despite the energetic and crowd-pleasing nature of the routine, which included a number of ambitious lifts, the technical aspects of the dance were found wanting by the judging panel 26, 29. Craig Revel Horwood, a judge known for his exacting standards, was particularly critical, likening Skinner's performance to a "weightlifting competition in Skegness" 1. The scores reflected this critique, with the couple receiving a total of just 13 points 1, 3, 7, 22. This low score, when combined with their week one total, gave them a combined score of 29, leaving them in a vulnerable position at the bottom of the leaderboard 1, 9, 18, 23.

The Dance-Off: A Final Performance

The combination of the judges' scores and the public vote resulted in Thomas Skinner and Amy Dowden finding themselves in the first dance-off of the series 1, 4, 6. They were joined in the bottom two by former England rugby captain Chris Robshaw and his professional partner Nadiya Bychkova 2, 5, 22. Both couples were required to perform their week two routines again in a final bid to impress the judges and remain in the competition 3, 15, 26.

Skinner and Dowden reprised their Salsa, while Robshaw and Bychkova performed their Viennese Waltz to "Die With A Smile" by Lady Gaga and Bruno Mars 2, 3, 15, 33. Following the two performances, the judges delivered their verdict. The decision was unanimous, with all four judges—Craig Revel Horwood, Motsi Mabuse, Shirley Ballas, and Anton Du Beke—choosing to save Chris Robshaw and Nadiya Bychkova 1, 2, 4, 5, 15, 22, 33. This sealed the fate of Skinner and Dowden, making them the first couple to be eliminated from the 2025 series 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 15, 22.

The Aftermath: Reflections and Reactions

In the moments following the announcement of their elimination, both Thomas Skinner and Amy Dowden shared their thoughts on their brief time in the competition. Skinner expressed his enjoyment of the experience, despite his self-confessed lack of dancing ability. "I've loved it," he said. "I've never danced before and my stay was short, but Amy's amazing. It's been great fun and I've enjoyed it. I can't really dance that well but I've had fun!" 1, 2, 5, 6, 15, 22, 35, 42. He also offered an apology to his partner, saying, "Thank you, Amy – sorry that we haven't done too good, 'cause you're a different class" 1, 2, 15, 22, 35, 42.

Amy Dowden's reaction was particularly emotional, given her recent return to the show. She spoke of the laughter and joy that Skinner had brought to their partnership, and how he had helped her to regain her confidence as a dancer. "The last three years as you know have been quite difficult for me," she explained. "I lost all confidence as a dancer - but walking into the room with you with a massive smile and a laugh, you brought me back and, honestly, thank you" 4. She also emphasised the importance of health and happiness, a perspective undoubtedly shaped by her recent experiences 32.

The public reaction to Skinner's elimination was mixed. Many viewers expressed their agreement with the judges' decision, with some commenting on the perceived poor quality of his dancing 14, 24. Others, however, voiced their support for Skinner, with some suggesting that he had been unfairly treated by the judges or that his elimination was politically motivated 34, 41.

Conclusion: A Fleeting Moment in the Spotlight

Thomas Skinner's time on Strictly Come Dancing was, by his own admission, short. However, his enthusiastic, if not technically polished, performances and his partnership with the returning Amy Dowden created a memorable, albeit brief, narrative arc in the early stages of the 2025 series. His elimination serves as a reminder of the demanding nature of the competition and the high standards required to progress. For Amy Dowden, her return to the ballroom, though ending sooner than she would have hoped, was a significant personal and professional milestone, demonstrating her resilience and her enduring passion for dance. The departure of the first couple invariably shifts the dynamic of the competition, intensifying the focus on the remaining contestants as they continue their quest for the coveted glitterball trophy.
